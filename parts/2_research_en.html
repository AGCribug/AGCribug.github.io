Computational Chemistry · Molecular Dynamics · Materials

Computational chemistry is a fascinating research method and its application fields are very broad. It includes quantum chemistry, first-principles calculations, molecular dynamics, etc.

Molecular Dynamics (MD) employs simple potential functions derived from classical mechanics to describe atomic interactions and to approximate the dynamic behavior of a large number of molecules. Typically, MD simulations are conducted on systems containing less than 2 million atoms and spanning timescales of less than 2000 nanoseconds.
